Imagine a world free of misinformation.

Politics has become extremely polarized. With the rise of the internet, people are increasingly disagreeing on the most basic facts. Trust in all of our institutions is low. Not only are people more misinformed, but they are also disinformed. People both have incorrect information to operate from and information which hurts their ability to find correct information. Fundamentally, this problem stems from a distrust of all institutions. People are increasingly going away from mainstream news outlets and towards alternative forms of media. While there are certainly problems with mainstream news outlets, alternative news is not an adequate substitute.

People’s beliefs form their actions. People’s beliefs form public policy. People’s beliefs form people’s lives. When beliefs formed on the basis of incorrect information pervade society, it harms people. Moreover, when institutions by which we can discern truth are called into question, it results in people being less likely to adopt true information. One of the most important institutions that informs society in all areas of science and politics is academia. While there are no doubt problems with academia, and legitimate criticisms people can make about it, academics are the people best able to discern fact from fiction. There has been a heavy assault recently on academia, to the point where many people do not regard it as having any worth to it. This phenomenon, better known as anti-intellectualism, is what The Spectrum Project aims to combat.

Combatting both misinformation and anti-intellectualism in today’s political discourse will allow people to once again have arguments based on fact, and not fiction. There can still be large disagreement even when people agree on the same facts. However, such a climate would be more conducive to effective political advocacy, action, and change. As explained before, one of the main problems with political discourse today is the lack of trust in today’s institutions. The Spectrum Project aims to be an unbiased, objective, and transparent resource by which people can obtain facts about pressing issues in today’s political discourse.

We are currently developing “fact sheets”. These will function as extremely transparent and unbiased resource sheets by which people can find out the facts about current issues. There will be multiple fact sheets – each focusing on a particle issue that is hotly debated today. There will be links to at least one source per paragraph which will direct you to a highlighted part of a webpage by an unbiased and/or academic source, to ensure that you don’t have to go digging for the facts. We believe that an effective solution to helping combat misinformation is to spread such fact sheets around the internet. See a politician tweeting lies about healthcare? Post a fact sheet in the comments. See a person on YouTube spreading misinformation about systemic racism? Post a fact sheet in the comments. Many social media platforms have already done this with regards to the coronavirus. We can do more. We live in a world characterized by what is called “post-truth politics”. We need discourse to be based on fact, and not fiction. Otherwise, we cannot have a functional society.
